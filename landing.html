Welcome to

Flood Thy Neighbor is a platform to explore people's attitudes towards flooding. Here, we gather the human perspective on the moral decisions in water resources management. We show you moral dilemas of managing floods, and you tell us what your preference is; which landscape should be flooded and which should be spared.





Flooding: In the News

Recent flooding across the midwest has brought greater awareness of the difficult decisions flood control and emergency managers must make when flood waters come and the potential for uneven damages to land and property is imminent. Many see the difficulty of river management under multiple, sometimes competing objectives. Others however feel that the efforts of flood control operations as insufficient.

These choice structures and sentiments have been chronicled by journalists in national and regional papers. The following is a short collection of some the work that has been done.

  • Flood thy neighbor: Who stays dry and who decides?

    "The Army Corps of Engineers has a huge, complex job – reducing flood risk across the nation’s rivers and coasts and a requirement to do it in a way that benefits the country economically... In practice, those formulas determine who gets flooded and who gets saved."
    - Lisa Song, Patrick Michels, and Al Shaw
    Reveal, from the Center for Investigative Reporting


  • The Fight to Tame a Swelling River With Dams That May Be Outmatched by Climate Change

    ... decisions affect the lives of countless communities and ecosystems -- the cities, factories and power plants that draw water from the river; the endangered species that nest on its sandbars; the farmers who cultivate its floodplains...
    - Tyler J. Kelley, NYtimes


  • Judge says Corps of Engineers is responsible for damaging floods along Missouri River

    "Under federal law the river is to be managed ... by balancing flood control, navigation, water supply, irrigation, power, recreation, water quality and wildlife preservation.""
    - Matt Campbell, Kansas City Star
